THIS IS SIGNN

The ASL to English Communication Assistant

 

This is what we do

 
 

Over 350,000 culturally Deaf Canadians experience difficulties in everyday interactions such as going to the bank or ordering at restaurants. Our team aims to bridge this communication gap with SIGNN (Sign-language Interpreting Gloves using Neural Networks). SIGNN is a portable, glove-based solution which leverages deep learning to process hand motions, interpret ASL phrases, and provide an auditory English translation in real time.

 
 
 

Current methods like typing on smartphones, or writing on notepads for translation are time consuming

Current sign language translation tools lack the portability needed to be useful in day-to-day interactions

meet the team

 

Rayyan Ghani - Electrical Designer

Rayyan is the electrical designer for SIGNN. His experience at Apple working on Apples Airpods has given him industry experience in owning and executing an electrical design end-to-end. He is responsible for the architecture, design, and validation of SIGNNs custom hardware solutions.

Archie Lee - Software Developer

Archie is a software developer for SIGNN. His experience working on machine learning models at NVIDIA has given him a deep understanding of neural networks. He handles the pipeline that enables our ASL-to-English translation network. This includes data collection and processing, training machine learning models, and validating our algorithms.

Kevin Huo - Embedded/Software Developer

Kevin is the embedded developer for SIGNN. His previous experience working on embedded systems at Apple and Microsoft HoloLens gives him a strong insight into the firmware design and development for the team. He focused on setting up the multi-IMU system on the gloves as well as developing the user facing Apple Watch application.

Jerry Li - System Architect

Jerry is the system architect for SIGNN. His experience working at Microsoft on Surface and HoloLens products has given him a broad range of knowledge in system integration. He creates the glue that connects the firmware, software, and hardware components together for SIGNN.
